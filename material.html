Cardboard


Cardboard is a generic term for heavy-duty paper-based products having greater thickness
Features : Absorption power, Insulation qualities (heat, sound, vibration), Air permeability and Shape stability

Water-proof: No

Durability: Strong & Resilient

Availability: Commonly Available

Cost: Affordable & Cheap(80-150rs per sqft)

Eco-Friendly: It is 100% biodegradable, minimizes waste generation,recyclable

Glass


Glass is an inorganic solid material,usually transparent, hard, brittle,& impervious to natural elements
Features : Transparency, Heat resistance, Pressure,Breakage resistance and Chemical resistance

Water-proof: Yes

Durability: brittle, can fracture

Availability: Commonly Available

Cost: Affordable,based on quality prices will increase

Eco-Friendly: Requires less energy, Reusable, Causes CO2 emmissions

Metal


Metal is a material freshly prepared, polished, fractured, and conducts electricity & heat relatively well.
Features :high melting points, high density, malleable ductile, strong and good conductors of heat and good conductors of electricity

Water-proof: Yes depending on usage

Durability: Hard & long lasting

Availability: Moderately Available

Cost: Mostly Affordable

Eco-Friendly: Not Sustainable, Alternative - Fiber Reinforced Plastic

PVC


Polyvinyl chloride is the world's third-most widely produced synthetic polymer of plastic. About 40 million tons are produced each year
Features :Less rigid, high impact strength, Is easier to extrude or mould, lower temperature resistance

Water-proof: Yes

Durability: Resistant To weathering

Availability: Moderately Available

Cost: Mostly Available(190-200rs per price)

Eco-Friendly: Not Sustainable,but is better than plastic & Metal

Paper


Paper is a thin sheet material produced by mechanically or chemically processing cellulose fibres, followed by pressing and drying.
Features :Weight, Thickness, Density, Two-sidedness, Smoothness, Permeability,rigidity

Water-proof: No

Durability: Easily Tearable

Availability: Very Common Available

Cost:Very Cheap

Eco-Friendly: Sustainable, Recyclale & Reusable but costs trees to be cut down

Rubber


Rubber is a tough elastic polymeric substance made from the latex of a tropical plant or synthetically.
Features :high tear and tensile strength, resilience to abrasion, friction, extreme temperatures, and water swell

Water-proof: Yes

Durability: Elastic, very durable

Availability: Moderately Available

Cost:Not too expensive

Eco-Friendly: Natural rubber comes from tapping plants, which makes it a renewable resource

Wood


Wood is a strong, material which comes from tree trunks. It is used for construncting things like furniture
Features :Sturdy, strong, light, floats on water

Water-proof: Some types

Durability: Quite durable

Availability: Widely available Available

Cost:Cheap

Eco-Friendly: It is quite sustainable because it is natural and biodegradable but trees need to be cut down in order to get this.

Leather


Leather is a flexible, soft material which comes from animal skin. It is used in furniture, bags, clothes and shoes.
Features :Flexible, soft, smooth, breathable

Water-proof: Water resistant

Durability: Very durable

Availability: Widely available Available

Cost:Expensive

Eco-Friendly: Leather is not very eco friendly becuase animals are killed to obtain it. However, vegan leather is a more sustainable option.

Ceramic


Ceramic is material made by firing at/baking clay. It is used in pots, tiles, mugs, construction, bricks, etc.
Features :Hard, strong, heat retaining, resistant

Water-proof: Most types are

Durability: It is quite durable but can break.

Availability: Widely available Available

Cost:Average

Eco-Friendly: Yes

Textiles


Textiles are woven materails such as cloth. They can be made from cotton, silk, wool and synthetics.
Features :Flexible, soft, light, breathable, fast drying

Water-proof: No

Durability: Depends on the type and usage

Availability: Widely available Available

Cost:Cheap - average (based on type)

Eco-Friendly: Mainly
